Cardio Budget

📊
3 incline walks/week max — Mon, Wed, Fri after lifting. Cap at 25 min. This burns ~200–250 cal each = ~600–750 cal/week from cardio.
⚠️
Don't stack cardio on top of a 500 cal diet deficit — total weekly deficit should be ~3,000–3,500 cal (1 lb). If you're eating 500 below maintenance already, 3 walks is plenty. Don't add more "just in case."
✅
Protect Lower days from all cardio — Tue & Sat are your hardest recovery days. Zero cardio, full stop.

🥩
Protein is non-negotiable — 145g minimum every single day, cut or not. This is what keeps muscle on while you lose fat. If you miss calories, don't miss protein.
🍚
Keep carbs around workouts — Eat most of your carbs pre- and post-lift. This fuels performance and replenishes glycogen without being stored as fat.
📈
Reassess at week 3 — If you're losing more than 1.2 lbs/week, add 100–150 cal back. Losing too fast = muscle loss. Scale should trend down, not crash.

Week 6 Deload

~June 2–8

Planned Deload Week

Keep the same weights on the bar, but drop volume by 30–40%. Do 2 sets instead of 3, cut reps to the low end of the range. This lets accumulated fatigue clear so you come into the final 2 maintenance weeks looking fuller, stronger, and less depleted. Do not skip this — it's when the visual result actually reveals itself.

Final 2 Weeks — Maintenance

June 8–20
🎯
Bump calories to maintenance — Add back ~300–400 cal mostly from carbs. You'll look fuller and more muscular as glycogen refills. This is not gaining fat — it's strategic.
💪
Keep lifting, reduce cardio — Stay on the same split but drop cardio to 2 sessions/week max. You want to look peaked, not depleted.
💧
Watch sodium 3 days out — Reduce processed/high-sodium foods 3 days before June 20. Don't do anything dramatic. Just eat clean and drink water normally.
😴
Sleep 7–9 hours — Wedding prep will spike cortisol. Poor sleep is the fastest way to look soft and flat. Protect it.
